Dedicated Team Model vs. Time and Materials vs. Fixed Price Model

When outsourcing your software development, you will have to decide on an engagement model that defines the basis of the collaboration between you and ValueCoders.

Dedicated Team

  • Mostly used for long-term projects with unclear requirements and potential changes in scope
  • It’s also common when the client doesn’t have the necessary skills to tackle the project at hand.
  • More affordable option than hiring an in-house team.
  • Team dedicated exclusively to the project at hand.
  • Continuous development and delivery for improved flexibility and scalability.
  • Faster workflow when compared to more strictly planned models.

Time and Materials model

  • Here you only pay for the time and effort the developers spend on your project working on predefined features and functionality.
  • You only pay for actual work performed on a specific timeframe
  • Flexible approach to development that gives room for changing requirements.
  • Highly scalable and rapidly adaptable to new needs.
  • Tight time management that ensures a faster development.
  • Increased cost control and budget flexibility.

Fixed Price Model

  • It takes place when the client and the development company agree on a fixed cost for the entire project.
  • You’ll pay only that predefined sum regardless of the amount of time or resources
  • No surprise costs and minimal client involvement as the final price is agreed before development starts
  • The workflow is predefined and agreed by both parties, meaning that there shouldn’t be any issues in delivering the project on time.

Dedicated Development Team Vs. Other Development Approaches

The difference between dedicated development team and other development approaches are:

  • Shared Resources: In traditional development approaches, resources are often shared across multiple projects, which can lead to conflicting priorities and reduced efficiency. In contrast, a dedicated development team is exclusively allocated to a single project, ensuring undivided attention and focus.
  • Alignment with Client’s Goals: Dedicated teams work closely with clients and align their goals and objectives, making them more invested in the success of the project. This alignment fosters better communication, increased ownership, and a deeper understanding of the client’s business needs.
  • Scalability and Flexibility: Dedicated development teams can quickly scale their resources up or down based on project requirements. This scalability enables businesses to adapt to changing needs without the hassle of hiring or terminating individual resources.

To know more about hiring a dedicated development team, read our detailed guide.

Benefits of Working With a Dedicated Development Team

The major benefits of working with a dedicated development team include the following:

  • Deep Domain Knowledge: Dedicated teams specialize in specific technologies and industries, allowing them to develop a deep understanding of the domain. This knowledge facilitates quicker problem-solving, better decision-making, and more innovative solutions.
  • Improved Productivity: With a dedicated team working solely on a project, productivity levels tend to be higher. The team members become familiar with the project’s intricacies, resulting in efficient processes, reduced rework, and faster delivery.
  • Effective Communication and Collaboration: Dedicated teams establish robust communication channels, ensuring seamless interaction between team members and clients. This open line of communication facilitates real-time feedback, fosters collaboration, and promotes transparency throughout the development process.
  • Cost Savings: While dedicated development teams require an initial investment, they can lead to long-term cost savings. The team’s efficiency, coupled with streamlined processes and reduced overheads, can result in optimized development costs over time.

Want to look for more benefits of hiring dedicated web development teams? Read our blog.

Skills and Expertise Needed When Building a Dedicated Development Team

When building a dedicated development team, the essential skills and expertise required are:

  • Technical Proficiency: The team should possess a strong foundation in the required technologies, programming languages, and frameworks relevant to the project.
  • Collaboration and Communication Skills: Effective communication and collaboration are crucial for the success of a dedicated development team. Strong interpersonal skills, active listening, and the ability to articulate ideas clearly are essential.
  • Problem-Solving Abilities: A dedicated team should be adept at analyzing complex problems and providing innovative solutions. Critical thinking, analytical skills, and the ability to troubleshoot are highly valuable.
  • Adaptability and Learning Agility: As technology evolves rapidly, it is important for a dedicated team to have a learning mindset, stay up to date with the latest trends, and continuously upgrade their skills.

Potential Challenges and Mitigation Strategy

With benefits come the challenges, however, they can be mitigated with the right approach. These include:

  • Time Zone and Cultural Differences: Establishing effective communication and collaboration can be challenging due to different time zones and cultural nuances. Mitigation strategies include implementing overlapping work hours, leveraging collaboration tools, and fostering a culture of respect and understanding.
  • Managing Remote Teams: When working with a distributed dedicated team, it is crucial to establish clear processes, set expectations, and leverage project management tools to track progress and ensure accountability.
  • Intellectual Property Protection: To mitigate the risk of intellectual property theft or leakage, it is essential to have robust legal agreements and confidentiality protocols in place. Non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) and secure data transfer protocols should be implemented.

Typical Process of Onboarding a Dedicated Development Team

The typical process of onboarding a dedicated development team includes the following process:

  • Defining Project Requirements: Clearly articulate the project goals, scope, and technical requirements to the dedicated development team.
  • Team Composition: Identify the roles and skills needed for the project and build a dedicated team accordingly, ensuring a balance of technical expertise and domain knowledge.
  • Selection and Onboarding: Screen and select team members based on their qualifications and experience. Facilitate a smooth onboarding process, providing access to necessary tools, documentation, and project-related information.
  • Establish Communication Channels: Set up effective communication channels, such as video conferencing, messaging platforms, and project management tools, to ensure seamless interaction between the team and the client.
  • Project Kickoff: Conduct a project kickoff meeting to align the team with the project objectives, deliverables, timelines, and expectations. Share project documentation and establish a shared understanding of the project scope and requirements.
  • Ongoing Collaboration: Foster regular communication, conduct frequent progress reviews, and ensure a collaborative work environment. Encourage knowledge sharing, provide constructive feedback, and address any challenges or roadblocks that may arise.
  • Performance Monitoring and Feedback: Implement performance tracking mechanisms to monitor individual and team performance. Provide feedback and recognition to motivate the team and foster continuous improvement.

By following these steps, businesses can effectively onboard a dedicated development team and set the stage for a successful collaboration.
