WooCommerce vs Shopify vs Magento- Which one to Choose?

  • Performance & Scalability

– MAGENTO

Magento is massively powerful and easily accommodates hundreds of products along with huge website traffic. 

If you are one of them who have a big business or are planning to expand the business then Magento can be an extremely efficient content management system for you. 

Magento is quite scalable and you can easily reduce or increase the resources your website needs depending on the traffic inflow. This literally means you don’t have to experience any barrier in website crashes or speed downfalls during a sudden change in traffic. 

– Shopify

In Shopify, when it comes to site performance & speed it fares well. In fact, website speed is one of the main benefits. 

Now talking about scalability, it does not perform well compared to the other two eCommerce platforms, which can prove to be a barrier if your business increases. If you need a smooth workflow for your business then you can go for Shopify plus. But this may also increase your cost heavily. 

– WooCommerce 

It allows scalability but people with less experience and no experience might find it difficult to map their websites according to the requirements. If you don’t have any coding backgrounds then you may find WoooCommerce scalable for you at all. 

So the winner is Magento 

Hire an eCommerce web development company to build a secure, and scalable website on the Magento platform.

  • Seo Effectiveness 

– MAGENTO

It offers advanced SEO features along with inbuilt efficient analytics. It generates SEO friendly meta description, URLs, and integrates Google site maps that are too auto-generated in websites and automates image resizing and watermarking. 

Along with these features, SEO can be smoothly personalized suiting your website requirements if you hold the required skill set. According to a study conducted in 2019, it was revealed that Magento had an SEO score of 100 out of 100. 

– Shopify 

Well,  being the first eCommerce store, Shopify is surely optimized for SEO but the only issue is that it doesn’t spare much room for personalization. There are various SEO practices available for your business but the problem is you won’t be able to deploy them as Shopify doesn’t allow. 

– WooCommerce 

WooCommerce has a plethora of extensions and plugins available that helps in a good degree of SEO. Users can easily tweak their settings as they like according to their website to make them better. 

So We have Two Winners here: Magento & WooCommerce

  • Reliability & Support 

– Magento 

Magento has a whopping community of nearly 3,00,000 members. With certified and amateur members in the team who are contributing to these communities, it is quite hard to search for help if you are facing any problems. 

– Shopify 

Shopify offers 24*7 support to its customers. The response time is very good and they are quite prompt with their solutions and replies. You can anytime reach customer service and connect through email, live chat, and phone calls. 

– WooCommerce 

WooCommerce is basically a WordPress plugin and you might be aware of the fact that the WordPress community is immense. You can smoothly run the platform, take the help easily, and avail yourself the numerous benefits from plugins and code created by developers in the community to improve your website functionality. 

So the winner is Shopify. 

  • Safety & Security 

– Magento 

In the case of Magento, safety & security is not a problem at all. If you have a team of efficient developers and hosting servers at your disposal nothing is as secure as Magento then. Further, Magento was the first eCommerce platform that kept the security of data as the priority. 

It offers security patches and there are a plethora of security plugins and extensions provided for securing the data. 

– Shopify

Since everything is globally hosted on Shopify, the platform is quite safe and secure. It keeps an eye on your server-side maintenance and timely regulates it. It also offers you a free SSL certificate and maintains PCI compliances for you. 

– WooCommerce

WooCommece keeps you updated all the time for bug security and fixing. The users need not worry about their security as the team working on the WooCommerce platform always monitors and alters the code to decrease such threats. 

In addition, there are also a plethora of security plugins available too that protect the website from every possible virus attack and hackers. 

So the winner is Magento

  • Ease of use 

– Magento 

When it comes to Magento, it has a high learning curve. One cannot maintain a website on this platform without getting a developers’ help or hosting service.

To avail the benefit of the Magento platform you should have the knowledge of cloud hosting, PHP, and HTML  or you can hire eCommerce developers otherwise it would be very difficult for you to run the website on Magento. 

– Shopify 

In the case of Shopify, I would definitely say it requires no prior skills of website development and coding. The platforms offer hosting and the software itself which means the consumers only need to design, manage and set up the store for which themes and plugins are available. 

– WooCommerce 

Well, if you are comfortable with the free version of WooCommerce themes and plugins then it is great for you. But, as I have seen, in the majority of cases, you will definitely need some customizations, after which WooCommerce will become tricky, mainly when you have no coding literacy. 

So the winner is Shopify
